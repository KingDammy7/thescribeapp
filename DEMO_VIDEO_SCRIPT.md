# The Scribe — Demo Video Script

A scene-by-scene walkthrough for you to record yourself (screen recording + voiceover). Each scene lists what to click and a suggested line to say. Total runtime aiming for roughly 4-6 minutes. Adjust the wording to your own voice — this is a guide, not a script to read verbatim.

## Scene 1 — Landing Page (15-20s)
**Show:** The landing page.
**Say:** "This is The Scribe — an AI writing assistant built specifically for apostolic and prophetic Christian authors. Instead of generic AI output, it learns your voice and writes in it."

## Scene 2 — Sign Up & Voice Interview (45-60s)
**Show:** Create an account, then start the voice interview flow.
**Say:** "Before you write a single word, The Scribe interviews you — about your theological emphasis, your tone, your signature phrases, the scriptures you return to again and again. This becomes your 'voice profile,' and it's what makes every chapter sound like you, not a chatbot."
**Action:** Click through 2-3 interview questions to show the flow, then show the autosave indicator.

## Scene 3 — Voice Preview (20-30s)
**Show:** The voice preview screen generating a sample paragraph.
**Say:** "Right after the interview, The Scribe generates a live sample in your voice, so you can confirm it's captured you correctly before committing to a full manuscript."

## Scene 4 — Dashboard (15-20s)
**Show:** Dashboard with stats and recent manuscripts.
**Say:** "The dashboard gives you an at-a-glance view: total words written, chapters completed, and your manuscripts in progress."

## Scene 5 — Creating a New Manuscript (45-60s)
**Show:** Click "New Manuscript." Fill in title, book type, purpose.
**Say:** "Starting a new book, you choose a title, a type — teaching book, devotional, memoir, prophetic manual — and describe its core purpose."
**Action:** Show the chapter count step — click a preset, then show the custom input.
**Say:** "You can pick a preset chapter count, or set an exact custom number if you already know your book's structure."
**Action:** Scroll to the cover style picker.
**Say:** "Then pick a cover design — these are designed templates with their own palette, font pairing, and layout, tied to the tone of your book. You can always change this later from the editor."
**Action:** Click "Generate Outline" to show the AI outline feature, then click "Create Manuscript."

## Scene 6 — Generation Mode: Chapter Focus (30-45s)
**Show:** Navigate to Generate mode for the new manuscript.
**Say:** "Before generating, you can tell The Scribe exactly what each chapter should focus on — the subject matter you want it centered on — so the AI doesn't drift."
**Action:** Click the pencil icon next to a chapter, type a focus, save it. Then click "Generate."
**Show:** Live streaming generation of a chapter.
**Say:** "Watch it write live, chapter by chapter, in your captured voice."

## Scene 7 — The Editor (60-90s)
**Show:** Open the manuscript in the editor.
**Say:** "This is the writing workspace. On the left, your chapter list — including your chosen cover. In the center, the manuscript itself, fully editable. On the right, an AI assistant you can ask to expand a thought, add a scripture, rewrite a paragraph, or just keep writing."
**Action:** Demonstrate: select text, ask the AI panel to "make this bolder"; drag-and-drop reorder two chapters; show live word count vs. chapter goal.
**Say:** "Chapters can be reordered by drag and drop, and you can track your word count against your goal as you go."

## Scene 8 — Scripture Studio (30-40s)
**Show:** Navigate to Scripture Studio, search a theme or keyword.
**Say:** "Scripture Studio is a searchable library of scriptures organized by theme — calling, warfare, identity, provision — so you can find the right verse and send it straight into your manuscript."
**Action:** Search for a keyword, show multiple results, click "Send to Editor."

## Scene 9 — Export (15-20s)
**Show:** Click "Export" from the editor.
**Say:** "When you're ready, export your manuscript as a document — ready to share or move into final formatting."

## Scene 10 — Settings & Theme (15-20s)
**Show:** Settings page, toggle dark/light mode.
**Say:** "And the whole app supports both dark and light mode, so it's comfortable to write in any lighting."

## Scene 11 — Mobile View (15-20s)
**Show:** Resize the browser window narrow, or open on a phone/tablet, show the hamburger menu opening the sidebar drawer.
**Say:** "Everything is fully responsive — the same writing experience adapts down to phone and tablet, including a collapsing navigation menu."

## Scene 12 — Closing (10-15s)
**Show:** Back to Dashboard or Landing.
**Say:** "The Scribe — write your book, in your voice, with an AI ghostwriter that actually sounds like you."

---

### Recording tips
- Screen-record at 1080p or higher; keep your cursor movements deliberate so reviewers can follow along.
- Record narration separately if it's easier to get clean audio, then sync in editing.
- Have a manuscript with at least one fully-generated chapter ready beforehand, so Scene 7's editor demo doesn't show empty content.
- Keep each scene's screen recording a few seconds longer than the talking point, so you have room to trim in editing.
